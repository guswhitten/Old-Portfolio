Description:

The point of modeling these various electrostatic problems in ANSYS Maxwell is to visualize electric field lines given a region of charge.

Infinite Line Charge Along Z-axis







The infinite line charge extends from positive to negative infinity in the +z and -z directions and has a finite charge. It has an infinitely small radius, making it a 2D concept. I chose to model the line charge as a 3D cylinder with 1000C charge in order to produce clean E-field lines.

Electric Flux Density of Parallel-Plate Capacitors

The point of this figure was to observe (and calculate) the effect of the fringing electric field lines on the capacitance of the charged plates.
The fringing field lines are the curved green/orange/yellow lines on the perimeter of the plates.
For detailed calculations on how the fringing lines add to the capacitance, see Lab 2 pdf below.
